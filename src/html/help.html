Thank you for registering with www.crewtimer.com.  Your account is now enabled for access to create your regattas and link your mobile device.  To get started simply enter your registered email address in the login box and log in.

Creating Your Regatta

Once you log in you'll see a new link on the bottom left of your screen labeled 'My Regattas'.  This is where you'll go to configure your regattas.  To configure a new regatta, click on 'My Regattas' and then 'Add New Event'.  You'll then have an opportunity to set your desired Regatta Name, Date, and other fields.

Creating Your Spreadsheet

Your most important entry will be the Google Doc URL.  This is a link to your online spreadsheet using Google Sheets.  The best way to get started is to start from a copy of two example spreadsheets:

  • CrewTimer example Sprint race
  • CrewTimer example Head race (this form can also be used for Sprint races)

Once you open one of these spreadsheets, select File/Make a Copy to start your own spreadsheet.  Delete or change rows to suit your needs.  Additional columns are OK and are ignored by CrewTimer.

If you have an Excel spreadsheet you can import this into Google Sheets and start from that as well.  The column headings should match the headers in the example sheet:

  • Sprint: Event #, Event Time, Event Name, Lane 1, Lane X
  • Head: Event Time,Flight,Event #,Event,Event Abbrev,
    Crew,Crew Abbrev,Bow #,Age

If you have a requirement to use a different header name for spreadsheet columns please let CrewTimer know as some header aliases are allowed.  e.g. 'Start' for 'Event Time' or 'Event No.' for 'Event #', etc.  As noted below, the download format from Regatta Central is also supported.

Connecting your Spreadsheet

To connect your Google Sheet to CrewTimer, a share link is copied from your spreadsheet and pasted into your regatta configuration.  To make the spreadsheet available to CrewTimer select the Share icon in the upper right, then select Get Sharable Link, then select 'Anyone with the link can view'.  Copy the share URL and paste into your regatta configuration page as your Google Doc URL.  This url is a private URL known only to you and CrewTimer.  It does not give CrewTimer permission to modify your spreadsheet.

Once you have entered your Google Doc URL click on the 'Load Spreadsheet' button on the My Regattas page.

Validate your setup by clicking on the CrewTimer logo in the upper left and selecting View Results for your regatta.

Configuring the CrewTimer mobile app

If you haven't done so already, download the CrewTimer app from the Play Store by searching for CrewTimer or use this link:
https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=net.entazza.crewtimer

Once you have the CrewTimer app loaded you'll need to enable the CrewTimer.com integration features:

  1. Select Settings Menu and check the Enable  www.CrewTimer.com integration option.
  2. Enter the Event Code shown on your My Regattas page.
  3. Enter the Event PIN shown on your My Regattas page.
  4. Exit Settings

The mobile app will contact www.CrewTimer.com and download the configuration of your regatta.  If you made a mistake on the Event Code or PIN, go back to settings and correct it.  If your regatta configuration does now show up, try exiting the app and restarting it.

Other

  • Use the Clear Race Data link to restart your regatta timing data from scratch.  Use this if you tested your mobile devices before the regatta and need to erase any time events.
  • Check the 'Public Visible' option to make your regatta visible to anyone on the Internet.  Without this checked, only you can see the results.
  • Users viewing the results from the Internet will only see results updated once a minute.  Click your browser refresh to see results sooner.
  • An Edit Results link will be visible.  This is an experimental feature and should only be used at the direction of a CrewTimer support contact.
  • PDF results are available for printing or archive by using the Chrome browser and selecting the Print icon.  From there select Save as PDF or Save to Google Drive.

Twitter Integration

If you wish CrewTimer to post to your twitter feed when races are delcared official, you can enable twitter integration on your regatta configuration page.  When you click 'Authorize Twitter' you will be taken to a twitter page to give permission for CrewTimer to post on your behalf.

Regatta Central download

CrewTimer supports the spreadsheet format produced by the generic spreadsheet downloaded from Regatta Central as boats.xls.  To integrate this spreadsheet with CrewTimer, follow the following steps:

  1. Download your boats.xls file.
  2. Import boats.xls into Google Drive
  3. Right click on boats.xls and select 'Open with Sheets'.  This will convert it into a Google Sheets spreadsheet instead of an excel spreadsheet.
  4. Edit your spreadsheet as desired and add bow numbers for all your entries.  If a bow number is not specified, CrewTimer will choose a number starting at 400 as a temporary number.
  5. Share your document as described above and paste the sharing url into CrewTimer.

If you download the RegattaMaster spreadsheet instead of the Generic form from RegattaCentral it will work as long as you insure the 'boats' sheet is first in the list of sheets.

Masters Handicaps

CrewTimer supports three Masters Handicap types as selected on the regatta configuration page:

  1. None - No handicap is applied for any entries.
  2. Manual - A column labeled handicap is applied to entries that also have an age specified.  The handicap is specified in seconds as a positive number that will be subtracted from the elapsed time of the entry.
  3. US - Handicaps will be applied to all entries that specify and age >= 27 using the formula specified by USRowing.  Entries with an age less than 27 will not have a handicap applied.

If you need a different handicap system applied, please contact CrewTimer with the details of your handicap system.

FAQ

  1. What is the '?' button for on CrewTimer mobile?  The question mark is for cases where you can't determine the bow number immediately but need to record one or more time events.  Once you press '?' a time event is recorded and you can subsequently edit the bow number for the time event by touching it.
  2. Can I hook up an external button for timing events?   Yes, you can hook up an external button to assist with recording timing events.  When an external button is used, the timing events are recorded on the button press and pressing bow numbers on the mobile device will match up to the recorded time events.  This is particularly helpful for close finishes.   For the external button, you can use a standard phone earbud with a microphone as used for handsfree operation as long as your handsfree device has a button on it.   A more ergonomic plunger style button switch is available from CrewTimer for $25.
  3. What if I accidently record an event?   Just tap the red X delete icon and mark the event as deleted.  Any event recorded by CrewTimer can be deleted in this manner.  Deleted events can also be restored.
  4. How do I assess a penalty?  Press and hold the bow number and select from either a standard penalty or a custom penalty used by your regatta.
  5. What if a boat does not finish or start?   You can press and hold the bow number to bring up a Penalty/Status form for that boat.  In this form you can mark the boat as DNS or DNF.
  6. What is the 'Official' button shown for each event used for?   This will change the online results page for the event from 'Provisional' to 'Official'.  If you have twitter integration it will also tweet a result message for the event.  If you accidently mark an event Official, you have one minute to mark it as deleted to avoid sending a tweet.  In other words, a tweet is not sent for one minute after you mark an event Official.
  7. What do the background colors of the timestamp mean?   Yellow/Orange indicates that the timing event has not been communicated to the CrewTimer cloud server.   Blue indicates the timestamp has been recorded in the cloud.  A red indicator means that there is a communication problem to the cloud, most likely due to poor 3G coverage.  This will automatically correct itself when communication is restored.  In any case.  all timing events are stored in persistent storage in the mobile device and will be communicated to the cloud server as soon as possible.
  8. What happens if I exit the app?  If you exit the app, all timing events are retained.  When the app restarts, any pending timing events will be communicated to the cloud server.
  9. Can I modify the spreadsheet after a regatta starts?  Yes, you can add entries, delete entries, and make any changes you like.  However, any entries for which there is already a time event recorded will be retained regardless of changes you make to the spreadsheet.   When you change the spreadsheet, CrewTimer will not update until you click the 'Load Spreadsheet' button.  Any connected mobile devices are also notified of the lineup change and the mobile UI will update as will any connected web clients viewing www.crewtimer.com.
